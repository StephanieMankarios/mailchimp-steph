I taught a meditation class this week around the practice of this Hawaiian form of reconciliation and forgiveness, so I thought I would share it with you because it has become one of my favorite meditations.

Similarly to how you would repeat a mantra (a word, sound, or statement repeated to aid concentration) over and over again during a meditation, the Ho'oponopono practice is the repetition of the following:

"I'm sorry

Please forgive me,

Thank you,

I love you".

With each of the 4 phrases, allow yourself to pause, and feel the words. Different thoughts or feelings might come up, allow them to arise. Let yourself feel.

It's a healing practice, and each time you repeat it, you might feel that the words are directed to a different person or living being. For example, you may be saying the words to yourself,

you may be saying the words to Mother Earth, or to a family member, or to an old friend.

The great thing about this practice is that if you don't feel like you have the time in your day to close your eyes and meditate, you can repeat this while you're cooking, or while you're driving, or doing any other task.

It can be taught to children.

And, it can be taught in any language or in any culture.
